Wood

The wood we use

Teak Wood

This wood is a first class wood because of its strength, durability and beauty.

Characteristics : Very Strong, Limited Materials

Mahogany Wood

The wood grain is fine and varied. This look is very good when finished with natural or classic colors.

Characteristics : Light, Easy to Fade, Reddish-brown color

Dalbergia latifolia Wood / Sonokeling Wood

This wood is also known to be very durable and almost rivals the quality of teak wood.

Characteristics : Heavy, not easy to fade, Blackish-brown color, some are dark brown.

Pine Wood

Pine wood is classified as rigid and not easily formed, durable, and strong.

Characteristics : Light, not easy to fade, Reddish-brown colos.

Frequently Asked Questions

What do you stain your woods with?

Absolutely NOTHING! All the vibrant color you see is 100% natural. Because of this, they are that same color all the way through the piece. If you take our suggestion and clean them with hot soapy water and the scratchy side of your sponge (which continuously removes the tiniest little layer of wood) then they will keep their color for decades!

How long will it take to get my order?

We have a full-time shipper here and we ship 5 or 6 days a week. If our website lets you place an order for a product, that means it is in stock and it will ship either the day you ordered it or the next day.

What is in the package?

We wrap each set or individual piece in cardboard, then include a wood care package and info card and handwritten note if you request it on the cart page. Then we wrap the whole bunch in kraft paper, seal it with an Earlywood sticker and put it in a soft envelope or box. We never include receipts so if you send it directly to someone as a gift, you don't have to worry about that.
